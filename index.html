Development Financing Sources in Pakistan (Million USD)

Key Insight: World Bank and IFC financing has increased steadily over the past 7 years, with IFC financing growing by 87% since 2017, demonstrating strong support during economic challenges.

Development Financing Growth (2017-2024)

Key Insight: IFC financing has shown consistent growth, with a notable acceleration in 2023-2024, reflecting increased private sector development initiatives post-pandemic.

Development Indicators Trends

Key Insight: After strong post-pandemic recovery in 2021-2022, Pakistan faced economic challenges in 2023 with growth slowing to 0.3%, followed by moderate recovery to 2.6% in 2024 supported by World Bank stabilization programs.

Social Development Progress

Key Insight: Renewable energy adoption has been the fastest-growing indicator, nearly doubling from 24% to 46% between 2017-2024, reflecting Pakistan's accelerated commitment to climate initiatives.

World Bank Group Sectoral Focus in Pakistan

Key Insight: Energy sector receives the highest investment (38%), reflecting Pakistan's focus on resolving energy crises and transitioning to renewable sources.

Sectoral Impact Assessment (1-5 Scale)

Key Insight: Energy sector shows the highest impact and sustainability scores, reflecting successful World Bank Group infrastructure investments.

Regional Distribution of World Bank Projects

Key Insight: Punjab shows the highest development impact score (4.7/5), while Balochistan scores lowest (3.2/5), highlighting regional development disparities.

Population vs. Project Distribution

Key Insight: Smaller regions like Gilgit-Baltistan show disproportionately high impact scores relative to population, demonstrating targeted development approaches.

Pakistan's Sustainable Development Performance

Key Insight: Pakistan outperforms regional averages in Renewable Energy and Digital Adoption, but lags in Gender Equality and Financial Inclusion.

Sustainable Development Progress

Key Insight: In Renewable Energy, Pakistan scores 75/100, significantly above the South Asian average of 63, reflecting successful World Bank green energy initiatives.
